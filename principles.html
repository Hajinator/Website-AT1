The principles of analysis and design involve understanding the requirements of a system, breaking down the system into manageable components, designing each component for efficiency and maintainability, and ensuring that the system meets the needs of its users.

Website architectural requirements include the structure of the website, such as its layout, navigation and how data is presented for the user. It involves choosing the appropriate technologies to ensure proper scalability, performance and maintainability.

Website design structures include the overall layout of the website, such as the footer, header, content areas and sidebars. Hierarchy refers to how the content is organized. Navigation design involves creating a clear and intuitive system for users to move through the site, using features such as buttons, menus and links.

User-Interface design requirements focus on creating interfaces that are easy to use and understand. The production process involves creating prototypes, user testing and refinement based on feedback.
